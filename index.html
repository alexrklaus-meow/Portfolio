About

I wasn’t diagnosed with my disability until I was in college. For most of school, I felt like a system that was supposed to help me had quietly decided I wasn’t worth the effort. Now I report on education because I want families navigating that same system to have someone in their corner who’ll tell them what’s actually going on. I sit through the long meetings, dig through the complicated data, and translate the bureaucratic lingo so you don’t have to.

Before Milwaukee, I covered Detroit schools for Chalkbeat and city meetings for Detroit Documenters, where I helped pilot its Docu-Mentor initiative. I hold a B.A. in Urban Studies and Public History from Wayne State University.
